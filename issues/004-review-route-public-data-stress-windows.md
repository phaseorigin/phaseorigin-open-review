## Purpose
This issue collects critical comments on one bounded CHC claim family.
It is not a request to accept the full CHC program.

## Claim family
Public-data stress tests (non-overturn / non-exclusion / bounded witness discipline).

## Standard tools retained
Standard dataset checks, likelihood reasoning, holdout-style reasoning, statistical controls, and domain-specific public-data constraints remain in scope.

## What CHC adds
Proposed labels and constraints designed to avoid overstating support, distinguish non-overturn/non-exclusion/bounded-witness results, and track boundary conditions explicitly.

## Failure condition
The route fails if labels are misapplied, indistinguishable from ordinary narrative, fail to prevent overclaiming, or cannot be reproduced from declared public-data inputs.

## Useful comments
- Mislabeling examples
- Better boundary wording
- Cases where the discipline fails in practice
- Missing dataset, likelihood, or survey-selection constraints
- Incorrect assumptions or boundary conditions
- Reproducibility or formalization issues

## Not useful here
- General agreement/disagreement with the full framework
- Promotional comments
- Unbounded speculation
